# Paralog Compensation Notes

## Why this matters

A weak or missing phenotype does not always mean the target is biologically irrelevant. Compensation by paralogs or related family members can mask the effect.

## Typical scenarios

- Knockout of one family member with a close paralog still expressed
- Stress-response rewiring after perturbation
- Developmental or activation-state compensation
- Context-specific dependency where only one system reveals the phenotype

## Useful questions

- Does the target belong to a well-known gene family?
- Are closely related family members expressed in the same cells?
- Is double targeting or orthogonal validation needed?
- Could the perturbation readout miss the compensated phenotype?

## Reporting guidance

- State whether compensation is likely, plausible, or unsupported.
- Distinguish family relationship from experimentally demonstrated compensation.
