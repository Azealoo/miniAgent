# Literature Synthesis Guidelines

## Goal

Summarize literature in a way that separates strong support, partial support, and unresolved disagreement.

## Rules

- Do not treat one paper as field consensus.
- Keep species, tissue, and perturbation context visible.
- Separate mechanistic claims from correlative observations.
- Prefer claims with explicit supporting citations.
- If evidence conflicts, show both sides instead of averaging them into a false consensus.

## Recommended structure

- Main question
- Strongly supported points
- Context-dependent findings
- Contradictions or unresolved questions
- Key representative citations

## Warning signs

- Only review articles and no primary studies
- Mixed model systems collapsed together
- No distinction between in vitro and in vivo evidence
- Claims stronger than the available data
