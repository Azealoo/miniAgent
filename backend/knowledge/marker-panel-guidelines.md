# Marker Panel Guidelines

## Goal

Build marker panels that help distinguish cell types, states, or activation programs.

## Good marker panel habits

- Prefer panels over single markers.
- Use both positive and negative markers when possible.
- Check whether markers are lineage markers, activation markers, stress markers, or cell-cycle markers.
- Consider species, tissue context, and stimulation state.

## Interpreting weak panels

- A panel with only one strong marker is rarely enough for confident annotation.
- Conflicting markers can indicate mixed states, doublets, contamination, or an unmodeled subpopulation.
- Housekeeping or very broad pathway genes are weak discriminators.

## Recommended output structure

- Proposed label
- Positive markers
- Negative markers
- Confidence level
- Main caveats

## Example caveats

- Markers overlap with multiple immune subsets
- Activation state may dominate lineage markers
- Ambient RNA or doublets may explain the signal
